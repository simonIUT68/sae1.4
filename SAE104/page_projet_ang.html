What job?

There are a ton of different jobs in the army, and I want to go into mechanised infantry and battle tanks in particular, It's a subject that has interested me for many years and has never ceased to fascinate me. Although it's a long way from reality video games introduced me to the 'discipline' of tank combat.

These are not simply metal boxes made to withstand fire, They are complex tools that, when used properly and with a coordinated crew, reveal the full complexity of combat itself, and that's why I'd like to make it my profession. To be more specific.

There are several positions within a combat squadron:
There are basic soldier members (shooters or pilots) who do not require any qualifications to access these positions and who manage the driving and shooting itself. the driving and the shooting itself.

There are non-commissioned officers (tank commanders) who coordinate the tank itself, tell it where to go, spot targets and report back to their commanders or receive manoeuvre orders. reports to his commanders or receives manoeuvre orders from them, and finally there are the officers (e.g. squadron leader) who coordinate a squadron of 15 tanks in relation to the other forces present and decide on the course of action to be taken. in relation with the other forces present and decides what action to take to achieve the objective given by his leaders.

How?

There are 3 main ways of achieving my carrer plan :

The first is simply to commit myself as a EVAT (Enlisted as a volunteer in the French Army) which means becoming a basic soldier, It's the least risky choice because you're sure to be accepted into the regiment you want.

The second option is to try the ENSOA, (National School for Active NCOs) in St - Maixent, which trains non-commissioned officers in 8 months, It's a rather risky choice because the first in the promotion chooses where he wants to go and the last takes what's left, In other words, the first ones get more or less the regiment they expect, and if they don't, they have to either try again or accept the post, so it's not certain that you'll get into the tanks.

The last choice is not the riskiest, but it is the least in line with my career plans. engineering school to become a network and telecoms engineer and then join the army as a Telecoms expert Officer (St Cyr), which isn't really what I want to do in the first place, but as I'm interested in these two professions, I'm not ruling out this possibility, subject of course to obtaining an engineering degree, which is also a risk. obtaining an engineering degree, which is also a risk.
